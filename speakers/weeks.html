Matthew Weeks

Matt Weeks serves as Director, Editorial Graphics Production. He is currently working with espnW as part of the ESPN's Job Rotation Program.

In his current role, Weeks oversees the Editorial Graphics Producer area in Production. His 22-person team is responsible for shaping and directing the editorial content appearing on graphics on SportsCenter, ESPNEWS, Baseball Tonight, SportsNation, Numbers Never Lie, ESPN Sports Saturday, Mike and Mike in the Morning, First Take, NBA Coast-to-Coast, Sunday and Monday NFL Countdown, NFL Live and NFL32.

Weeks joined ESPN in November 2004 as a Graphics Producer. He was promoted to Senior Manager of Integrity in October 2006, and he was part of the management group that launched the Stats & Analysis Team. He rejoined Production as an Associate Director in December 2007.

From 1999 to 2004, Weeks worked at USA TODAY where he served as a desk editor and writer. At the McLean, Va.-based newspaper, Weeks regularly contributed pieces on professional, college and high school sports. He primarily covered Major League Soccer and the U.S. men's and women's national teams. Weeks covered the 2003 FIFA Women's World Cup.

Weeks also worked at The Kansas City Star from 1998 to 1999 as a sports writer, assigned to The Star's Johnson County bureau in Overland Park, Kan.

Weeks started his career at The Washington Post in 1996, where he wrote about professional, college and high school sports. Weeks also served as a Page Editor and Layout Editor at The Post.

Weeks graduated from George Mason University in 1998 with a Bachelor of Arts in Speech Communication.
