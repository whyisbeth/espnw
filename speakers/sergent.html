Talley Sergent

Director of Strategic Initiatives
U.S. Department of State

Talley Sergent is the Director of Strategic Initiatives for the Assistant Secretary of Educational and Cultural Affairs at the U.S. Department of State. At the Department, she spearheads efforts to elevate public diplomacy programs focusing on empowering women and girls. In addition to her role at the State Department, Talley serves as an international advance lead for the White House, traveling with the President, First Lady, Vice President, and Second Lady of the United States. Prior to her service at the State Department, Talley served as State Director for West Virginia and National Press Advance for Hillary Rodham Clinton's historic campaign for President. A veteran of political campaigns, she began her career on Capitol Hill working for U.S. Senator John D. Rockefeller IV of West Virginia. Talley graduated with a Bachelor of Arts in Journalism and Mass Communication from the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill and is a native of Huntington, West Virginia. She is a loyal Cincinnati Reds and Bengals fan, and believes the sky is only one color: Carolina blue.
