Mary O'Connor

Vice President of Olympic Marketing and Global Platforms for Omnicom’s The Marketing Arm (TMA)

Mary O’Connor serves as the Vice President of Olympic Marketing and Global Platforms for Omnicom’s The Marketing Arm (TMA).

In her current role, she leads TMA’s Olympic Marketing division, developing and executing integrated Olympic marketing initiatives for agency clients. In addition, O’Connor oversees several agency accounts, including Hilton Worldwide, Oracle and BP, and leads the sales efforts for TMA’s global platforms and hospitality initiatives.

She has a proven track record of delivering award-winning, integrated marketing programs that engage consumers, B2B customers and employees, truly demonstrating the impact that sponsorships can have on a brand when leveraged correctly. She is most well known in the industry for her experience across all facets of Olympic marketing, including contract negotiations, strategy, activation, hospitality, NGB partnerships and athlete endorsements.

In the summer of 2008, her team delivered one of the most successful and impactful domestic activation programs of the Beijing Games. The program “AT&T Team USA Soundtrack” raised $1 million for Team USA athletes and won 9 industry awards, including 3 PMA Reggie Awards, 3 Promo Pro Awards, 2 MAAW Globe awards and 1 Adweek Buzz Award. In March 2009, O’Connor was named to the Sports Business Journal’s “Forty Under 40” list for her leadership of the Soundtrack program.

Most recently, O’Connor and her team completed an eight year deal between Hilton and the Chinese Olympic Committee (COC), making Hilton the first multinational hotel company to sponsor the COC.

Prior to joining TMA in 2005, Mary spent time at both Carat North America and General Motors. Over the course of the last thirteen years, her work has positively impacted several noteworthy brands including AT&T, Hilton, BP, Oracle, General Motors, Schick, The Home Depot and The New York Times.

O’Connor earned an MBA in sports and entertainment marketing from Indiana University’s Kelley School of Business and a bachelor’s degree in business administration from the University of Notre Dame. She also completed the Omnicom University Senior Management Program.

In an effort to give back to her community, among other things, O’Connor is a “Wish Granter” for the Make-A-Wish Foundation in greater Dallas and is actively involved with the Pancreatic Cancer Action Network (PANCAN). She also serves as an ACP mentor, a nationwide program dedicated to assisting recently returned veterans with their career development through mentoring and networking.
