Tina Johnson

Editor-in-Chief, espnW

In February 2011, Tina Johnson was named editor-in-chief of espnW, ESPN’s newdigital launch targeting female athletes and female sports fans. Johnson joined espnW from the market leading Women’s Health magazine where she was founding editor-in-chief.

At espnW Johnson oversees the multimedia team responsible for creating compelling content to reach active, sports-minded women. With her successful record in brand and multimedia development, Johnson is responsible for setting the editorial direction for all of espnW’s digital content offerings, including mobile and social media platforms.

Previously, Johnson was executive editor for Teen People capping a 20-year career at Time Inc., a tenure that included 14 years at People Magazine in New York, Los Angeles and Sydney, Australia. In 2004, she was recruited to Rodale Inc. where she was editor-in-chief of the award winning Women’s Health. Johnson’s background also includes several consulting engagements with Hearst Magazines and Reader’s Digest.

Johnson is a graduate of Vassar College. She and her team are based at ESPN’s Bristol office. She resides in New York and Connecticut.
