Erika Carlson

Mental Skills Coach

She specializes in working with elite youth female athletes. Her work focuses on helping her athletes improve overall performance and enjoyment of their sport by empowering her them with mental skills such as managing self-talk, building confidence, controlling nervousness, setting goals and learning imagery. She has worked in the field of Applied Sport Psychology since 1998 and is the Founder of her firm Excellence in Sport Performance. She provides comprehensive services for athletes in addition to workshops for coaches seeking to learn how to incorporate mental skills into their training. She also teaches sport parents how to develop ideal roles to best support their young athletes. Erika has worked in every mainstream sport, but specializes in elite female soccer players.

Erika is one of approximately 300 people in the nation to be a Certified Consultant by the Association of Applied Sport Psychology (CC-AASP). She earned her Masters Degree in Sport Psychology from John F. Kennedy University (JFKU) in 2000 and completed a thesis on the Burnout Syndrome in sports. She earned a B.S. in Psychology from Colorado State University in 1995. Following her graduate studies, Erika worked for JFKU as a Supervisor of Graduate Students, and has taught classes including The Psychology of Coaching, Theory and Practice of Sport Camps. Additionally, Erika was a lecturer at San Jose State University where she taught Stress Management to Undergraduates.

Professional engagements include:

  • Presenter, International Conference for Science, Education, Medicine in Sport, Glasgow, Scotland, 2012
  • Presenter, Association for Applied Sport Psychology Annual Conference 2009, 2010, 2011
  • Presenter, Children’s Hospital Oakland Sports Medicine Pediatric Update –2009, 2010, 2011
  • Presenter, 49ers Foundation “Think Gold” Conference –2010
  • Lecturer, San Jose State University Kinesiology Department
  • Supervisor of Graduate Students, John F. Kennedy University

Erika is also a competitive athlete. She continues to compete as a 20-year veteran equestrian and also competes in road cycling time trials, and triathlons. Erika has 3 kids ages 12, 10 and 8.
