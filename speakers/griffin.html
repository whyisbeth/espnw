Mimi Griffin

President & CEO, MSG Promotions, Inc.

Mimi Griffin has owned and operated MSG Promotions, Inc. since its inception in 1983. Her extensive experience in the sports and event management fields has facilitated MSG’s successful execution of many prestigious events. By placing an emphasis on service, clear and constant communication and attention to detail, Mimi has distinguished MSG Promotions as one of the finest companies within the event management field.

This outstanding reputation was recognized in 2009 when Mimi was selected as one of Pennsylvania’s Best 50 Women in Business. It has also been recognized by the United States Golf Association, who selected MSG to manage the corporate hospitality sales, fulfillment and operations for the 2001 through 2018 U.S. Open Championships. MSG has generated hospitality revenues for the U.S. Open in excess of $150 million since 2001. Mimi has also served as Executive Director for the 1995 and 2009 U.S. Women’s Opens and 1992 and 2000 U.S. Senior Opens .

Mimi’s valuable experience in sports and event marketing and management has come from many different sources. She worked as ESPN’s primary women’s basketball analyst from 1983 through 1999 and with CBS Sports from 1985 through 1991. 1n 1990, Mimi became the first woman to serve as a color analyst for a men’s NCAA tournament game on ESPN and in 1991, was the first woman to broadcast the first two rounds of the men’s NCAA basketball tournament on CBS Sports. This accomplishment is noted in the Basketball Hall of Fame in Springfield, Massachusetts. Mimi served as the studio analyst along with Robin Roberts for ESPN’s coverage of the NCAA Women’s Basketball tournament during the 1996 through 1999 seasons

Mimi graduated with a Bachelor of Arts in Economics from the University of Pittsburgh and Masters in Business Administration from Lehigh University. After completing her degrees, Mimi worked in the Special Events Marketing department at Manufacturers Hanover Trust (1980-82) in New York City and later accepted the position of National Director of Promotions for Women’s Athletics for Converse (1983-85). She resides in Allentown, PA with her husband Bill and two children Kyle (24) and Casey (19).
