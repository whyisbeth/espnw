Sue Hovey

Former VP and Executive Editor, ESPN The Magazine

Sue Hovey is a former vice president and executive editor at ESPN. She spent 14 years with ESPN The Magazine, and most recently served as editor-at-large for espnW, directing content for the company’s 40th anniversary salute to Title IX. (She left ESPN this past summer to work as an independent editorial consultant.)

In her five years as executive editor with The Magazine, from 2006 to 2011, Sue helped guide all aspects of editorial strategy and execution. She oversaw the creation of the Body Issue and managed The Mag’s NEXT franchise; she also directed, at various times, coverage of the NFL, MLB, soccer, motorsports, college basketball and the Olympics.

Sue was drafted by ESPN in 1998 as a founding member of The Magazine’s launch team. Prior to that, she spent seven years as a writer and editor at Folio: magazine, covering the magazine business. She graduated magna cum laude from Syracuse University, with a dual degree in magazine journalism and classical civilizations.

A proud native of New Hampshire, Sue grew up obsessing over the Red Sox, Celtics, Patriots and Bruins. She also played four years of varsity basketball, softball and volleyball. Today, she makes her home in Brooklyn, where she enjoys running in Prospect Park and playing co-ed softball.
