Barb Lazarus

Owner and Founder of Game On!

Barb is the owner and founder of Game On! Sports Camps 4 Girls, multi-week summer sports day camps just for girls. With a mission that includes empowerment through exposure to and development in a multitude of sports, girls of all athletic abilities, grades preschool through 10th, experience hours of pure fun with the opportunity to grow as an athlete and a person. The program stresses quality and offers a unique and spirited camp culture.

Game On! is about supporting sports opportunities for girls. It's about inspiring both the recreational & travel athlete. It's about building confidence so that young girls have the chance to fall in love with and appreciate sports and fitness for the rest of their lives. It's also about changing attitudes, even if one family at a time, in order to contribute to growing the fan base for women's collegiate and professional sports. Game On! was born out of real passion.

Currently, Game On! is poised for strategic expansion around the country by partnering with sports organizations, companies, and/or with former and current collegiate and professional athletes who are looking for a career path that combines passion, the opportunity to give back, and the chance to become entrepreneurial.

Barb graduated with a B.A. from the University of Michigan (1982) and a J.D. from Loyola School of Law (1985), after which she served as an Assistant United States Attorney in the U.S. Attorney's Office, Northern District of Illinois, Chicago office. She went on to grow her own thriving media relations business for over 12 years before shedding her corporate clothing to pursue her dream, “ Game On! - and the chance to give back to girls sports. Barb is also the author of Game On! Sports Girl Talk, a blog addressing issues relating to the participation of young girls in sports and fitness.

Barb is the proud mother of two and continues to be active in both sports and fitness. The Game On! website is located at www.gameonsportscamp.com. The Game On! blog, Sports Girl Talk, is accessible through the website.
