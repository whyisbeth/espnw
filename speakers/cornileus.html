Tonya Cornileus Ph.D.

VICE PRESIDENT, LEARNING & ORGANIZATIONAL DEVELOPMENT

Tonya Cornileus is the vice president, learning & organizational development at ESPN, where she serves as an integral member of the senior human resources leadership team. Cornileus is responsible for the global learning, talent management and organizational development strategies for ESPN employees around the world. She also serves as an advisor to senior management at ESPN on all matters pertaining to her field.

Prior to joining ESPN, Cornileus served as director, executive development & organizational development for Turner Broadcasting System, Inc. from 2004 to 2009 and vice president, training & organizational development for Aegis Communications Group, Inc. from 1998 to 2004. She began her career as an educator in various school systems from 1985 to 1996.

Cornileus earned her Ph.D. in Adult Education/HR & Organizational Development from the University of Georgia in 2010. In 2004, she earned her M.Ed. in Human Resources and Organizational Development from the University of Georgia. She graduated with a bachelor’s degree in Telecommunications from the University of Florida in 1985.

Cornileus is involved with several professional and civic organizations, including the Executive Champion for the New England Chapter of Women in Cable Telecommunications (WICT). She has served as an active leader and presenter with WICT, the National Association for Multi-Ethnicity in Communications, the Society for Human Resource Management, the American Society for Training and Development, the Adult Education Research Conference, the National Association for Multi-Cultural Education, and the American Association for Adult and Continuing Education, just to name a few. Cornileus was recently selected to serve as part of the speaker faculty and advisory committee for the Corporate Learning and Development Institute and recognized as one of the CableFax 2012 Most Influential Minorities in Cable.

In addition, Dr. Cornileus serves on the Board of Directors for the Urban League of Greater Hartford and actively supports the Boys & Girls Clubs of Hartford and the Children’s Aid Society of New York.

She currently resides in West Hartford, Connecticut.
